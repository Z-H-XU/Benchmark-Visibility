# Multi-Horizon Highway Visibility Forecasting from Traffic Video via Physics-Informed Deep Learning

Thank you very much for your interest in our work.

## Overview

Road visibility analysis from traffic video remains a challenging and under-explored problem due to the lack of high-quality datasets and reliable ground truth measurements. In our previous work, we addressed this challenge from a perception perspective, focusing on visibility estimation from video frames using physics-guided feature representations and deep learning models.

## Published Work (Visibility Estimation)

This work has been published in:

> Yuexia Wang, Linyin Zhou, and Zhihuo Xu  
> *Traffic Road Visibility Retrieval in the Internet of Video Things through Physical Feature Based Learning Network*  
> IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems, vol. 26, no. 3, pp. 3629–3642, March 2025  
> https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10817150  

## Current Work (Visibility Forecasting)

Building upon this foundation, our current research extends the problem from visibility estimation to multi-horizon forecasting, representing a fundamental shift from passive perception to proactive decision support in intelligent highway systems.

Our new work, titled:

> **Multi-Horizon Highway Visibility Forecasting from Traffic Video via Physics-Informed Deep Learning**

is currently under review.

### Key Contributions

- Physics-informed feature representation derived from atmospheric scattering mechanisms  
- Improved physical baseline model for visibility persistence  
- Deep temporal models (PI-DLSTM and PI-DConvLSTM) for multi-horizon forecasting  
- Systematic evaluation for 15, 30, and 60-minute prediction horizons  

This transition from estimation to forecasting is not an incremental extension but a substantial advancement, enabling visibility to serve as a predictive indicator for:

- Proactive warning  
- Adaptive traffic control  
- Risk-aware decision-making  

## Repository Purpose

To support research in this field, this repository provides:

- A benchmark dataset for traffic video visibility analysis  
- Implementation of physics-informed feature extraction  
- Deep learning models for visibility estimation and forecasting
